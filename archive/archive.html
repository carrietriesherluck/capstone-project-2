Simplified Coursework

To ensure educational equity, schools tend to assign shorter, easier to read works, allowing for everyone to receive a quality education. This means that less advanced reading skills are taught.

Additionally, even if a student fails, they have multiple opportunities to retake exams and redo assignments.

04

Falling Behind

Especially in marginalized districts, libraries lack access to interesting books, and students have limited guidance regarding selecting suitable books.

Learner’s poor health conditions, learners with special needs, learners who are convinced that they cannot succeed in reading fall further behind, and a lack of teacher training converges to shape lower average literacy skills.

Reading Motivation Declining...

Educational systems intended to create equity and success may unintentionally discourage reading motivation and weaken literacy cultivation.

Part 05

Rebuild the School System

Activate each policy card to watch reading conditions, confidence, and access improve across the dashboard.

Introduce higher academic standards that prioritize skill mastery, rather than complete equity, and measure progress through literacy tests. Introduce support systems like training teachers in how to teach reading better, so students will be able to read, and thus like reading.

Introduce merit-based teacher salaries where teachers are measured by their performance and student performance outcomes

Beginning in kindergarten and the first grade, school must cultivate dedicated reading environments and diagnose students with reading difficulties and implement intervention programs at an early age to ensure that reading confidence is built.

Schools should expand the amount of books available in libraries and provide guidance on how to select suitable books. This will allow students to have a variety of books to choose from and determine which one of those best suits them, building confidence and increasing autonomy

Utilize AI Conversational Agents in reading instruction and reading practice, allowing students to answer comprehension questions, clarify any misunderstandings, or have someone to talk to during this reading process. This allows children to focus their attention, express their thoughts and critically reflect on the topic being discussed, which increases interest in reading and allows for a deeper engagement with the source material.
