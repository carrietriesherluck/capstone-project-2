Part 01

What Is Happening?

Technology captures attention and personalizes feeds → attention and autonomy decrease → sense of time weakens → mental exhaustion increases → users feel too mentally exhausted to begin slower, intentional activities like reading

Left Side / Research Quotes

"College students tend to spend extensive time scrolling short-form videos despite being aware that this leads to procrastination and delayed sleep."
"Platforms foster compulsive engagement patterns structurally similar to gambling."

Right Side / Continuous Partial Attention

Continuous Partial Attention (CPA)

Definition: Constant monitoring of multiple streams of information without deeply focusing on any single one.

  • fragmented focus
  • constant scanning
  • reduced sustained attention
  • cognitive overstimulation

The Reveal

Did you notice all the ways technology tried to capture your attention?

The distractions were not random. They mirrored common Attention-Capture Damaging Patterns designed to keep users following the platform's path instead of their own goal.

FOMO Alerts

"You missed 12 new updates"

The feed implied that leaving meant losing access to something important.

Emotional Headlines

Ragebait posts

Exaggerated emotion pulled attention faster than reflection could evaluate it.

Autoplay Reels

Videos kept continuing

Movement and sound cues competed with the slower intention to read.

Infinite Comments Scroll

Endless replies

New comments created the feeling that the conversation was never complete.

Engagement Notifications

Likes, comments, messages

Micro-rewards interrupted the search for START READING.

Time-Wasting Challenges

Pointless quizzes

Low-value tasks still demanded clicks, decisions, and mental energy.
