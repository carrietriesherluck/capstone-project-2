Research Lens 04 / Social Identity

Social Lens

Reading is not just a private habit. It is shaped by gender expectations, peer belonging, family confidence, and what a community decides counts as reading.

The Insight

The social story behind who becomes a reader

Gender Gap

45% of boys read less than once a month, compared to 27% of girls. Meanwhile, 43% of girls identify as avid readers, while only 30% of boys do.

Reading Identity

Many students read constantly online, but do not call themselves readers when only print books are treated as legitimate.

Choice Gap

Students are more likely to read when they can find books that reflect their interests, communities, and emotional investment.

The Paradox

Students widely acknowledge that reading is beneficial, yet a significant portion of them almost never read for leisure.
